About

I enjoy crafting high quality furniture from wood. I strongly believe it's the deatils that separate a piece of mass produced furniture from one handcrafted with care. Some large details and some small. Things that keep the furniture long lasting, like dovetailed drawers, pinned mortise and tenon joinery, and using solid wood for construction. Custom sizing is also important, as most generic furniture is built to a few general sizes. Most homes are not, so finding a piece of furniture to fit a specific nook or cranny can prove difficult. Even large pieces of furniture benefit from custom sizing to the room or space that they reside in.

There is also the aesthetic details that separate bespoke furniture from mass produced furniture. These are the details you may not necessarily notice at first, but lead to the overall aesthetic and look of the piece. Things like grain selection; continuous grain running across drawer fronts, book matched panels and straight grain on legs. Inlay, banding and high quality figured veneers are also high end details that really take a piece over the top.

I enjoy all aspects of woodworking and furniture making. From finding lumber stored in an old barn for many years, to selecting the perfect rough cut wood for a project. Seeing it come alive as you mill it and remove the rough cut marks, cutting the joinery. Finally applying a nice finish that really showcases the piece.
